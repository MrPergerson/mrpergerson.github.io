ALGAE BANDIT

Released: 2020
Medium: Video Game
Tools: Unity
Type: Collaborative work

Algae Bandit is a game about being a rogue crab who has chosen to steal food from other crabs. As the player, you get to choose how you want to steal food from crabs, by either using stealth or beating up any crab in the way. This game was developed during the summer of 2020 and was part of the Game Dev Best Practices Workshop, which was hosted by the Game Dev Club at SJSU.

Overview

  • Planned 1-week sprints with well-defined tasks by following the SCRUM methodology.

  • Adjusted the development calendar to adapt to the results from sprint reviews and playthings.

  • Listened to teammate needs and adjusted plans to help them.

  • Keep team meetings focused on core problems and check-ins short.

  • Algae Bandit achieved over 70 page visits and 30 plays on Itch.io within the first week.

My Role

As the producer I lead a team of two programmers and one artist. My responsibilities included creating and tracking tasks, running sprint evaluations and check-ins, and setting team milestones. We started preproduction in early June and our goal was to finish the game by August, before school started. Using the SCRUM framework as a guide, I ensured that our development process was iterative and communication was flowing.

Results

Within the summer of 2020, we started with a small idea and finished a playable and polished game. We presented our game at the last day of the summer game dev workshop and within that week, we got 70 page visits and 30 plays. Almost a year later, there are over 200 page views and over 100 plays.

Lessons Learned

  • Test all elements of the game as early as possible.

  • One-on-one meetings are important for checking in with teammates, especially if they are feeling stressed or unhappy about their role.
