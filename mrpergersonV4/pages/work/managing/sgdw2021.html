SUMMER GAME DEV WORKSHOP 2021

The summer game development workshop is an interdisciplinary group based project that focuses on game development. Teams are formed from the arts and animation, computer science and other disciplines interested in game development. These teams create a prototype over the 8-9 weeks of the workshop within the community of participants. We organizers focus on production, pipeline, design, prototyping, presentation, marketing and player feedback to create the most viable, fun prototype in the limited time. Participants are encouraged to experiment with genre, style, mechanics and any other aspect of game development within the team setting.

Overview

  • Taught a workshop, with two other SJSU professors, focused on game production and playtesting.

  • Devised a team forming method that supported different types of people.

  • Developed a game design document template and example for learning.

  • Meet with game designers and programmers groups to discuss issues.

  • Brought in over 60 artists and programmers and formed over 10 interdisciplinary teams.

My Role

As a workshop lead, I worked with two other San Jose State University professors to plan and lead workshop meetings and assist members. I wrote multiple slide outlines, a game design document outline, and a Trello template. I assisted members by reviewing their design documents, trellos, and game progress. I designed the website and advertisements to get people to sign up.

Results

Our workshop attracted over 60 artists and programmers. 10 interdisciplinary teams and 2 independent developers were formed shortly after.

Lessons Learned

  • Planning for team forming needs to happen far in advance and all data that needs to be taken in needs to happen early, all at once.

  • Learning how to manage tasks, get time estimates, and managing sprints is just as important as ensuring that members feel that they are contributing to the team.
